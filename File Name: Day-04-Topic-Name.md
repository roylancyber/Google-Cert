# Day 04 - CISSP Security Domains and Common Cyber Attacks

## The Eight CISSP Security Domains

CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional) is a globally recognized cybersecurity certification awarded by ISC² (International Information System Security Certification Consortium).

The eight CISSP security domains are:

1. Security and Risk Management

   * Focuses on governance, compliance, policies, and managing organizational risk.

2. Asset Security

   * Focuses on protecting organizational data and assets.

3. Security Architecture and Engineering

   * Focuses on designing and building secure systems.

4. Communication and Network Security

   * Focuses on protecting networks and data transmission.

5. Identity and Access Management (IAM)

   * Focuses on controlling user identity and access to resources.

6. Security Assessment and Testing

   * Focuses on evaluating security controls and identifying weaknesses.

7. Security Operations

   * Focuses on monitoring, responding to incidents, and maintaining security.

8. Software Development Security

   * Focuses on integrating security throughout the software development process.

## Common Cybersecurity Threats and Attacks

### Malware

Software designed to damage or compromise devices and networks.

### Computer Virus

Malicious code that interferes with computer operations and damages data or software.

### Password Attack

An attempt to gain unauthorized access to password-protected devices, systems, networks, or data.

### Phishing

The use of digital communication to trick users into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

### Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing attack directed at a specific person or group while appearing to come from a trusted source.

### Business Email Compromise (BEC)

A phishing attack where a threat actor impersonates a trusted individual or organization to gain financial advantage.

### Social Engineering

A manipulation technique that exploits human error to gain private information, access, or valuables.

### Social Media Phishing

An attack where a threat actor gathers information from social media to create a more convincing attack.

### Vishing

Voice phishing that uses phone calls or voice communications to steal information or impersonate trusted sources.

---

## Physical and Hardware-Based Attacks

### Physical Attack

A security incident that affects physical environments in addition to digital systems.

### Physical Social Engineering

An attack where someone impersonates an employee, customer, or vendor to gain unauthorized physical access.

### USB Baiting

An attack where a malicious USB device is intentionally left for someone to find and plug into a computer, infecting the network.

---

## Advanced Cyber Attacks

### Cryptographic Attack

An attack that targets secure communication methods between a sender and receiver.

### Supply-Chain Attack

An attack that targets software, hardware, systems, or third-party providers to exploit vulnerabilities and distribute malware.

### Adversarial Artificial Intelligence (AI)

The manipulation of AI and machine learning technologies to perform cyber attacks more effectively.

---

## Important Terms

### Hacker

A person who uses computers to gain access to systems, networks, or data. A hacker can have malicious or ethical intentions.

---

## Personal Takeaways

* Cybersecurity is a broad field that includes risk management, networks, software, and human behavior.
* Many attacks target people through social engineering rather than only targeting technology.
* Understanding different attack methods helps security professionals detect and prevent threats.
* The CISSP domains provide a framework for understanding the major areas of cybersecurity.
